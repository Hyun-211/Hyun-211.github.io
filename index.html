About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at the Research Center for the Early Universe (RESCEU), Department of Physics, The University of Tokyo, supervised by Prof. Jun'ichi Yokoyama, and a JSPS Research Fellow (DC1).

I study gravity from both phenomenological and theoretical perspectives. My research began with inflationary cosmology, with a particular focus on the observational consistency of the R² inflationary model, reheating, and baryogenesis. Recently, I have been exploring scattering amplitudes and their applications to black hole physics, including quantum effects for black holes, Hawking radiation, and gravitational memory.

Research

Black holes from on-shell amplitudes

Quantum effects for black holes, black hole thermodynamics, and local detailed balance derived from spin universality and unitarity.

Hawking radiation & black hole scattering

Effects of Hawking radiation on black hole scattering, and gravitational memory in black hole mergers from amplitude methods.

R² (Starobinsky) inflation

Cosmological evolution after R² inflation: reheating dynamics, the role of the Higgs field, and observational consistency.

Baryogenesis & primordial black holes

Baryogenesis scenarios in the reheating era and the possibility of primordial black hole production after inflation.
